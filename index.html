How to make a CAMEL

Facts About Camels

Camels can live around 40 - 50 years.

Newborn camels don't have humps.

Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

How to make a CHAMELEON

Facts About Chameleons

Chameleons have eyes that can swivel.

Chameleons have a lightning-fast tongue.

Sick chameleons look pale in color.

How to make a CICADA

Facts About Cicadas

Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies.

Most have red-orange eyes.

Females lay 200 to 400 eggs in tiny holes that they make in the branches of trees and shrubs.

How to make a PANDA

Facts About Pandas

Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.

Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

How to make a PIGEON

Facts About Pigeons

Pigeons can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.

Pigeons can fly at the speed of 50 to 60 miles per hour.

Pigeons commonly live up to 15 years and sometimes longer.

Facts About Bears

Bears are related to walruses, seals and sea lions.

In the north, bears sleep in the winter, but they don't hibernate.
